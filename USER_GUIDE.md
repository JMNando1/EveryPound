# Every Pound — User Guide

Welcome. This guide shows you how to use the monthly money tracker to see exactly where your income goes and stay accountable to your own savings goal. It comes in two forms and you can use either or both:

- **The app** — installs on your phone, works offline, best for logging spending as it happens.
- **The spreadsheet** — best if you prefer a laptop, formulas, and a printable record.

Everything you enter stays private to you. The app saves only to your own phone; the spreadsheet lives only on your own computer. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.

---

## Part 1 — The App

### Installing it on your phone

You don't get this from an app store. You open its web address once and add it to your home screen.

**On iPhone (Safari):**
1. Open the app's link in **Safari** (it must be Safari, not Chrome).
2. Tap the **Share** button (the square with an arrow).
3. Scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**, then **Add**.
4. The icon now sits on your home screen and opens full-screen like any app.

**On Android (Chrome):**
1. Open the link in **Chrome**.
2. Tap the **⋮** menu (top right).
3. Tap **Add to Home screen** (or **Install app**), then confirm.

After installing once, it works with no internet connection.

### First-time setup (two minutes)

1. Tap the **⚙︎ settings** icon (top right).
2. Enter your **monthly take-home income** — the amount that actually lands in your account.
3. Pick your **currency symbol** if it isn't already £.
4. Close settings. You'll see a starter list of categories (Rent, Groceries, Savings, and so on).

### Making the categories yours

- **Rename** any category: tap its name and type over it.
- **Change a budget:** tap the number under **Budget** and enter what you *plan* to spend or set aside for that category each month.
- **Add a category:** tap **+ Add category** at the bottom.
- **Remove one:** tap a category's name, then tap the small **Remove category** link that appears.

You only do this setup once. Your categories and budgets carry into every future month automatically.

### Using it each month

This is the daily habit that makes it work:

1. When you spend or pay something, open the app and put the amount in that category's **Actual** column.
2. The **Left** column shows budget minus actual — **green** means you're within budget, **red** means you've gone over.
3. The **allocation bar** near the top redraws live, showing what share of your income each thing is eating.
4. The **Left after everything** figure at the top is your real remaining money for the month.

Move between months with the **‹** and **›** arrows around the month name. Each month keeps its own actuals, so last month's record stays intact.

### Your savings goal

1. In the **Savings goal** card, tap **Edit**.
2. Enter your **target amount** (for example, 10000), the **amount you've already saved** as a starting point, and a **target date**.
3. Tap **Save goal**.

The app adds up the money left over in each month you've recorded and shows how close you are — and tells you roughly how much more you need to save each month to hit your target on time.

### Backing up your data

Your data lives only on this one phone. If you change phones or clear your browser, it's gone unless you back it up.

- Tap **Back up my data** to download a small file. Keep it somewhere safe (email it to yourself, save to cloud storage).
- On a new device, install the app, then tap **Restore from backup** and choose that file.

### Common questions

**Is my financial information stored online?** No. It never leaves your device. There's no account and no server.

**What if I clear my browser or delete the app?** Your data is erased. Back up first (see above).

**Can two people share one installed copy?** No — each person should install their own from the same link. Their data stays separate because it's stored per-device.

## Part 2 — The Spreadsheet

The spreadsheet does the same job with formulas and a printable layout. It has several tabs:

- **Assumptions** — every figure the plan is built on. Cells in **blue** are the ones you can change; everything else calculates from them. Change a blue cell and the whole workbook updates.
- **Salary Structure & Targets** — shows what percentage of your income goes to each area (giving, housing, debt, living, savings) against sensible target percentages, and how much is left after everything.
- **Monthly Plan** — the month-by-month projection of income, outgoings, and running balances.
- **Month Template (DUPLICATE ME)** — a blank monthly tracker. To start a new month: right-click the tab → **Move or Copy** → tick **Create a copy** → rename it to the new month.
- **The dated month tabs** — one per month. Fill in the **Actual** column (the shaded cells) as money moves; variance colours itself green or red automatically.
- **5-Year Projection** — the long-range view.

### How to use it each month

1. Open that month's tab.
2. In the **Actual (£)** column, enter what you really spent or saved in each row.
3. The **Variance** column colours green if you're on or under budget, red if over.
4. The **Notes / Where it went** column is for your own record of any unusual spend.
5. At the bottom, **Money Left After All Identified Deductions** shows your true surplus, and the **Cash Savings Closing Balance** carries into next month.

### One rule to keep it honest

Enter *every* pound somewhere, even small or awkward spends — there's an **Other / Unplanned Spending** row for anything that doesn't fit a category. The tracker only tells the truth if nothing is left out.
